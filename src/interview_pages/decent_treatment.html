Dean Reynolds AKA Decent Treatment

March 6, 2026

Who is Dean Reynolds AKA Decent Treatment?

Dean Reynolds AKA Decent Treatment is a horror RPG developer and illustrator. They also notably host the Electric Airship Interview Series, which focuses on topics in RPG design. On November 3rd of 2024, their team SEMICOLON released a Definitive Edition of the enigmatic psychological thriller BLOOD ORANGE. The game features light puzzles, disturbing atmosphere, unnerving dolphins, a mystery to unwind, oppressive atmospheric music, and beautifully rendered pixel art. Now we're standing at the precipice of a new title, THERMOCLINE, which seems to be a full-on horror JRPG. I sat with Dean to learn what they've been up to, their personal influences, what we should expect from THERMOCLINE, as well as the scoop on their now decade-long solo project THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT.

And we're live!

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): I'm Hunter, owner of the site foryoureyesonly.nekoweb.org, I'm here with Dean Reynolds of Decent Treatment. Why don't you go ahead and just introduce yourself for the audience real quick.

Decent Treatment (Dean): I'm Dean Reynolds, also known as Decent Treatment. It's just kind of like a screen name. I make video games, paint, and do a lot of visual art in general.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): What what part of the video game? The whole process? Are you more involved with one part or another?

Decent Treatment (Dean): A little bit of everything. The only thing I don't do is music.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): You've got your hand in pretty much almost every step of the process, it sounds like.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): As far as you mentioned, decent treatment is kind of just a moniker. I was actually under the assumption that it was moreso a game studio.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Oh, There's something like that. So SEMICOLON is a team of like 30 people. I'm kind of like the 'director' of Semicolon-- I do most of the programming, the art direction, and stuff like that. But there are many people involved in that project. They're the super awesome team from BLOOD ORANGE and the upcoming THERMOCLINE. We've got lots of specialists-- a sprite artist, a sound designer, and a composer, and many other people.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Okay, so it sounds like you have a whole team. So you're doing a lot of the the work yourself, but you're also kind of leading an effort?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, I'd say so.

Like, if I completely had my way with BLOOD ORANGE, it would be a bit different. Like, yeah-- it was my idea to do the dolphins, right? But I also kind of wanted to go into this weird esoteric stuff... There are these people that are super into dolphins and how they have these crystal palaces; it's a conspiracy theory.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, okay. Like a secret civilization.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah. The dolphins, people believe they're so big-brained that they kind of live in another dimension, like a DMT dimension or something. Things like this are hard to get by a team. But I did get the dolphins in at least.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Ah, okay. Yeah. I feel like that's such a major component of the game, so I'm glad it's in there. The dolphins themselves come off as extremely creepy, and unnerving. I feel like it's a very core part of the experience, so

Decent Treatment (Dean): Thank you. Appreciate it. Yeah, I drew those. (Aside from the dolphins in the cover art).

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): When you say writing though, are you specifically meaning plot points and direction of the story, or are you talking about dialogue or some mix of the two?

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): When you say 'make that work in video-game form', what are the considerations coming to your mind when you're looking over dialogue for a video game?

Decent Treatment (Dean): A big thing is like... I picked a really restrictive font where I could only have like 15 words or something on screen at once. So I kind of have to shrink everything down. So it's distilling everything down to poetics and then also making sure that it works as an interactive dialogue tree.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, sometimes limitations like that can lead to some creativity. Not always a bad thing.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, yeah, for sure.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Very cool. Pulling back away from BLOOD ORANGE for a moment, I wanted to talk a little bit more about you personally. What got you specifically into game design? Why did you start making games? What drives you to make games?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Mm-hmm. That's a great question. What got me into it and what drives me nowadays are two very different things. There's just so much potential in video games. When I when I was a kid, I played a lot of Secret Mana on Super Nintendo. It's this action RPG with stellar art direction. The way you interface with the game feels so natural-- it feels like you're in an actual alternate world, you know? That was a big inspiration for me. So in elementary school, I started making games on printer paper for my friends. I remember-- on the playground, there was this little gazebo next to the the the soccer fields. We'd sit down there and I had this little envelope full of characters my friends had drawn to play as. I mean there were no mechanics, my games were all bullshit. It was always dark, and edgy. And from there I was like... 'Yo, what if I made an actual game?' So I spent a lot of time as a kid making stupid title screen images. Like, what if I made a game that was called 'The Game', you know? I have a bunch of these old title screens that I made in paint.net. And honestly, they're pretty fire still. And so this sort of broader worldbuilding thing started to sprout in my mind. Even before I was making video games, I started working on these ideas, like a few of the core deities in this universe. That's all kind of stuck, to some degree, in my personal work. So this isn't like SEMICOLON, but my solo work.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Are you referring to THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT?

Decent Treatment (Dean): THRONE, yeah.

Decent Treatment (Dean): I've been working on that for... I mean if you really want to get technical and nitty-gritty about it, many concepts in that game came from my elementary school era. At some point I was playing Neopets on my old brick of a laptop, and they showed me an ad for RPG Maker XP. I begged my mom to buy it for me, but I ended up cracking it because my mom was like, 'no, I don't have money to buy a stupid video game for you.' I must have cracked RPG maker 20 times during my childhood. And so I just made stupid video games. Like that's the only way I could motivate... at some point I was just so zonked off in my own world... that's the only way I could get school assignments in, making video games and then recording them for my teachers. I've just been making games this whole time. Yeah, so that's how I that's how I got started. I don't know if there's a more succinct way to say that. 'It's in my blood, man.'

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, yeah. It's... you're a natural.

Decent Treatment (Dean): I don't know about 'You're a natural', but at least I've been I've been thinking about this stuff for a while.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): I really vibe with a lot of what you just said, but for music. So I get it. I was cracking FL Studio as a kid. I was just making up dumb little melodies and beats in my head when I was like six, but I had no way to record it. So I get it. You could say you've been doing it since you were just a kid. Now, I'm more interested in capturing interesting corners of human psychology. Or simply expressing my self, rather than some aesthetic value. If I can get somebody to be like 'oh shit, that really fucking hit me.' And that came from an image in my life? That's what really gets me going. Otherwise, I like the idea of pushing technical boundaries.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Okay. Yeah. We can get into that if you want. I'm not gonna shy away from anything technical, so...

Decent Treatment (Dean): Okay. Sweet. I mean I'm still using RPG Maker. So nowadays, I really enjoy doing things that nobody has ever done before in the program.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Not an easy feat.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah. So in Thermocline, we have a thermoregulation system. There's real-time temperature, which also affects stealth and battles in a sort of dynamic way. And hey, nobody's ever done that before in RPG maker because it's a real pain in the ass.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): There's no there's no tutorial floating around online on how to affect your battles with a thermostat.

Decent Treatment (Dean): That's for sure. Even more broadly, it's not something people have designed turn-based RPGs around at all. It's very experimental. I like that stuff.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Okay, yeah. Really playing with mechanics, just seeing what you can do, stretching traditional RPG aspects into almost a new format. That's cool.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Perhaps in a similar realm, I did want to ask why it seems that there's a bit of a focus on the horror genre. What drove you in that direction?

Decent Treatment (Dean): I love horror. It acts as a gateway for people to consider deeper topics. You wouldn't search 'how to process my trauma' on Steam but 'scary dolphin game'-- maybe! So we kinda sucker punch people. It's easy to inject these psychological themes and morals into a horror game.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, that does track. I'm a big horror fan myself. When I find myself playing horror games, a lot of them handle differently, that realm of dealing with the psychology of characters. Horror is a vast genre though. It can really be a lot of things. Specifically with BLOOD ORANGE and what I've played of THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT, it seems you're very honed into that specific psychological flavor of horror. You're not making slashers or party games like Phasmophobia or anything like that.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah! I make psychological thrillers, for the most part.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): These personal philosophies or just like things you're thinking about on a day-to-day basis-- Are you putting a bit of yourself into the game?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Mm-hmm. Yeah, absolutely. Yeah, a good example of that-- I got into this huge car crash. Like 70 miles an hour on the freeway, we flew over the center divide and flipped over four or five times. It was really bad. We landed sideways. I look over, and my passenger is just sort of hanging there. They've got what's called 'nystagmus', essentially their eyes were very mechanically darting back and forth. I had to get them down out of the seat belt, so she wouldn't die or become paralyzed, and I had to hold her there, wading in gasoline, standing on the window of my car until somebody arrived with the jaws of life. Anyways, my point is-- in BLOOD ORANGE, where do you think the the darting eye on the dolphin comes from? Came from some serious fucking trauma, you know? Where do you think the fucking car crash sound effect comes from? It's not like I was recording the car crash, but that channeled a very specific moment in my life, definitely. Absolutely, I express myself.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): That's a very, very literal take on putting a piece of your soul into what you create. That's heavy, but it's created some good art.

Decent Treatment (Dean): I mean, that's where the best art comes from-- the the moments only you can talk about.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Exactly. No holding back.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Outside of video games themselves, are there other interests, other hobbies, things you're into, that influence the games you're working on?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Oh definitely. I love painting and there are definitely tons of painters that I look up to. They sort of influence the games I work on, tonally. You know, like big Edvard Munch fan. I love Lucian Freud, like I could probably list out a ton of other modernist painters... And like, just exploring! I like to go out on hikes. I'll see something weird on the hike and I'll be like, 'That looks like an object with a Dark Souls lore description'. The other day, I was walking around at the park and I sat down at the bench and there's just a lock of hair on the ground. Somebody might have cut it off of their head and left it there. That's so conspicuous. Where the hell could that have come from? What are the lore implications for this?

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, there's gotta be a bit of a story behind that.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah. Like some rite of passage! Or maybe some guy that just had scissors and decided to cut all his hair off. Something happened.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): And so when you're out on those hikes, you're seeing stuff and you're thinking, Why is it that way? What made it? What's the story behind it?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah. I love looking at graffiti or carvings on trees. Why did they say this weird existential thing, like, on the wall of a storm drain?

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Right, yeah. Yeah, a lot of people don't see those things, amid the daily routine. It's interesting when you do stop to actually think about why is that there.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, it's good to just take some time for yourself and absorb the world. Let it wash over you.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah... It's not as common these days... everything moves so fast, we've all got little computers in our pockets, people don't...

Decent Treatment (Dean): There's too much hustle and bustle!

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, people don't take the time to sit down and just analyze their environment, what it really is that's surrounding them.

Decent Treatment (Dean): I definitely feel that, like everybody's got work all the time. Slowing down is the yin to my yang... going out and just walking around in nature.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): It's a good way to relax. I try to do it, but I probably don't do it enough.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, same.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Transitioning back to BLOOD ORANGE, I did have a few more questions I wanted to dive into there. I was wondering, what was the reasoning behind choosing a free-to-play model?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Well, the games that really got into my head as a kid were all free. I played a lot of free games on the internet, like Crypt Worlds and just random games on Game Jolt. There's this one weird game... it was a King of the Hill themed pinball game. It was also kind of a horror game and it was called Hawnk Hell. And I found that so profound as a 13-year-old.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): I'm gonna have to search that up.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, it's a great experience. I wonder if it holds up today? Probably not. But they made sequels, it was great. To me, video games have always been somewhat of a democratic medium. Like zines. You make a zine, you hand them out, sell them for a dollar, right? It's about getting your idea across. And yeah, I like that far more than commodifying the art I make. Eventually I'll have to make some money on this stuff, but you know, it's worked out for for a while.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): The art direction for BLOOD ORANGE. It's a very selective color palette.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah. Yeah, it was kind of... almost a Game Boy influence, but then we broaden that with some more oranges and blues.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): What made you go with that design? 'Cause often times you see pixel art but its usually pretty colorful, like very bright and in your face. It's not often you see pixel art that's dulled.

Decent Treatment (Dean): That's the thing. A lot of these video games, they don't really think about "how do I make an interesting piece of visual art?". Interesting art on some level comes from limitation. So the easiest way to do that, in my opinion, is by limiting your color palette and just challenging yourself to use that restricted palette. Yeah, I like the idea of just having a really strong orange focal point among desaturated green.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Is there anything that you're hoping people take away from BLOOD ORANGE? Or is it kind of just, take whatever you want?

Decent Treatment (Dean): It's a 'take whatever you want' situation. We left it intentionally vague. Like I said, I put my heart and soul into this, but that doesn't necessarily mean I'm trying to pull you in one way or the other. It's more... my powerful imagery works in cohesion with our intentionally vague narrative. And yes, we do have a very specific intention behind the whole thing, but we want everybody to have their own interpretation. There was somebody who did a interesting analysis of BLOOD ORANGE recently-- they were like, 'Nora is in the closet and the whole thing is her inner conflict with being in a lesbian relationship' and it's like whoa... that's not what I thought it was gonna be, but it exists that way for that person and I think that's beautiful.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, I'll be honest, I actually did not realize, for I think a good chunk of the game, that I was playing a woman nor that the the lover was also a woman at first. ...Different identities, different people playing the same game are always gonna have a unique takeaway. To me, their lesbian relationship almost went over my head.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): But I want to talk about your big project, THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT. I think it's your solo project, if I'm not mistaken?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT! Been working on this for ages! And yeah, it's a solo project. I mean, there actually is one person that's helped me a bit with sound design. Alexander Bruyns, they are super talented. And Louis Morel also helped me a bit with with a few sound effects, but overall THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT is me.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): And it's still ongoing in development, correct?

Decent Treatment (Dean): I put it on a short hiatus to work on THERMOCLINE. But yeah, THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT is my baby, and there's no fucking way I'm gonna fucking not put that out. I need to put it out. There's no way. I've been talking about this for ages! This is like my whole fucking thing. You have no idea. THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT is coming out, and it's coming out soon, and it's gonna be smoking hot. It's gonna be so fucking good.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): I don't know if you remember this, but I actually did play... I guess you might want to call it a beta, years back.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Oh yeah, like pre-alpha, like triple pre-alpha.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): It was available publicly as a demo at the time?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, it was available online as a demo. At some point I was just like, yeah, this game's too fucking big now. It's too different from this demo. So I took it down. Maybe I'll put the original demo back up at some point just for posterity or whatever. But for now it's down, until I get a more updated demo up.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): You could leave it as a piece of lost media. I might have the files still.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Literally, yeah. Somebody found it like last year or something and posted a playthrough on their big fancy blog, and they were like, 'this game is coming!'. And I'm like, 'thank you, but I wish you didn't post that because that's like 10 years old or something'. Thank you though. You gave me a lot of good feedback on that back in the day.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Oh wow, I barely remember that, but I do remember enjoying it. I remember the the art style was all hand drawn by you, no?

Decent Treatment (Dean): That's right. Yeah.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): It was fairly impressive. Kind of grotesque but in a good way. Everything had this very grim feel about it. I'm sure it's changed a lot since I last saw it though. Do you wanna give a brief overview of the game for people unfamiliar?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Most definitely. I never get an opportunity to talk about this! So THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT is this badass video game I've been working on for upwards of 10 years or 20 years if you include the elementary school escapades. There's these tarot cards which you find throughout the world which you can use to essentially change your class completely mid-battle. That's my really cool battle mechanic. And it's also cool because it's a turn-based JRPG, but it's all about predicting your enemies' moves. So where in something conventional like Final Fantasy, you're given maybe some states to manage, or a guy blows up your party, and that's your whole problem. In THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT, you have one character and you're expected to respond with a diverse and sometimes random array of tools. There's more of an emphasis on exploration and finding the things to solve whatever roadblock is ahead. So it's more of a nonlinear experience. Another cool thing about THRONE is there is there's a 24 hour real-time time limit. So every time you die, you lose an hour, because I hate gamers. And basically, you can't die 24 times or you just lose the game completely. THRONE is my baby. THRONE rocks.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): One influence very obvious both in the atmosphere and I guess the difficulty is Dark Souls, right?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Huge, yes, Dark Souls 1. Absolutely.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): I actually have not actually played that. I have a little bit of time on Dark Souls 3 and then a bunch of time on Elden Ring, which is technically not even Dark Souls.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Oh it's a classic. Dark Souls 1 though, the appeal of that game is that it's this 'small open world'. It's almost like a Metroidvania where everything's sort of interconnected, and it feels like everything connects in a very logical way, like a puzzle box. That's one of the things I really like about it.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Right, yeah. Is that something that you're trying to replicate in THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Absolutely. Yeah. Everything takes up a logical place in the world. You don't walk into somewhere and then all of a sudden like... You're on a different part of the island. Everything takes up a logical tract of space. For example, any of the entrances or exits to a dungeon will lead out into the overworld in exactly the corresponding place. It's about immersion. It's about simulating a world.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): You mentioned the tarot card system and switching classes. Can you go a little more into that? I'd love to hear that more about that.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, absolutely. Absolutely. So essentially during battle, you can pull an array of three cards-- a 'past, present, future' spread. You can then pick one of these cards. So there's randomness, but order within this randomness. So say you pick The Magus card, right? The cool thing about this class is that you lose 10 body every turn and gain 10 mind. So essentially it puts this really fast clock on on doing whatever cool thing you want to do. You might even want to switch to another card if your body gets too dire. The other thing that's interesting about these classes-- if you invest three turns in any one of the classes, you can use a ultimate move. So you focus for three turns, and then they each on the 22 major arcana have a unique ultimate. So like one ultimate is you don't have any mind cost for anything. One of says is anything you do, you'll do it twice. And they may seem simple, but when you're using five things at once, they stack in an interesting way. All of the sudden, players feel like they've broken the game to their will.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, emmergent gameplay, right?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Totally. That's where I really feel like I've got something cool. And it's just been a lot of banging my head against this system for a really long time. I honestly don't know how the hell I got it this cool, but in THRONE, there are a million ways to dismantle any of the bosses. And gosh, there's one move where you can rewind time on the values of your HP or your MP. One of the tarot cards, the Hermit, encourages you to with a significant damage boost to stay at give or take 10% of your maximum body. So you just have to avoid all damage completely, but then you do a ton of fucking damage. These are somewhat unconventional for a turn-based JRPG. So I'm very proud of myself in that regard. I hope players enjoy the 'toy box' I give them.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Mm-hmm. I've always felt like when playing RPGs, and I've played quite a handful, it was too simple of an equation, in battle. There is a pattern to follow and once you figure it, you just follow that pattern until you win. But it sounds like you're really trying to break out of that.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, absolutely. I'm tired of these grilled cheese JRPGs where you know you can just press A and win every fight.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, or the worst you gotta think about is like, should I buff right now? Maybe use a potion?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Exactly. Yeah. It's so milk toast. We need actual game mechanics in our RPGs. So be the change you want to see in the world, I guess.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): It's a little tangential, but I do feel like maybe the last... decade or maybe just the last five years... it's kind of been the age of indie games. So we get to enjoy lots of new innovative ideas in the video game space right now.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, absolutely. Like there's plenty of people like... Have you heard of Scumhead? They did Vomitoreum and most recently Mohrta. Mohrta is this low-poly crazy Doom/Souls-like thing. It's really great. You can tell that he loves the stuff that he's making. If you're talking about indie developers, that guy is really cool.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Yeah, I think the best part of the the entire indie game movement is that people aren't in it for the money. So you know they're coming with their best ideas. You know they're passionate. You know they're trying whatever the hell they want. They're not worried about 'Will this sell?' They're just thinking 'I want to make this.'

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah. Absolutely! Have you played Darkwood? It's so good, and it's better than like 90% of the survival horror games out there.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): No, but it's on my list. I'll get around to it eventually! In a similar vain, I really enjoyed Signalis.

Decent Treatment (Dean): I gotta like double down and just play that all the way through. I like the CRT effects and the Parasite Eve perspective.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Definitely recommend you check that out. I feel like it'd be up your alley.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): I did want to ask, do you have any idea how far along THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT is?

Decent Treatment (Dean): The systems are all done. I got some pretty big areas, so I just gotta kinda tie it together and call it a day. But I gotta finish THERMOCLINE first, that's what I'm focusing on for now.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Is there anything that you hope players get out of THRONE: FINAL TWILIGHT?

Decent Treatment (Dean): That's a good question. I hope they get a portrait of my individual perspective on the world. I hope they get immersed into this fantasy realm that I've been working on for fucking ages. I'll avoid getting any more specific than that.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): Do you want to shout out where people can find that game when it's ready to be released?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Oh yeah, everything is on my linktree.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): So we we can move over to THERMOCLINE, our big main topic. The page on Steam... It's vague. I get a sense maybe that's on purpose.

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yes.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): What can you tell me about Thermocline, I expect maybe there's some stuff you don't want to share yet. Leave it for the the gameplay experience, but...

Decent Treatment (Dean): THERMOCLINE is emotionally and spiritually a survival horror RPG. I'm really, really proud of the pixel art that's gone into it. A lot of it was me, but there were many artists involved. Like for instance, an amazing illustrator known as BadAlias did most of the creature designs. That guy is a legend. It was amazing working with him because he'd just be like 'Here's five creatures!', and it's like, 'What? How did you do that?' Keep an eye on that guy. My friend Paula did the character design. She goes as Chifle on the internet. And she's super cool. So how the main character looks or how the people you interact with look-- that's Paula. So the story-- You're on this abandoned planet, right? You've just crash-landed. So you gotta figure out a way to get off or survive amid this this frigid wasteland.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): What does it actually entail? You're on this planet, and you're trying to survive and you have these resources and there's some creatures, but how how does it actually play?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Right. So, you've played Silent Hill or like Resident Evil. You're familiar with the looping dungeon... you find the item and then that allows you to do a puzzle. There's a lot of that sort of thing. And there is more non-linear level design as well in the exterior area.

For Your Eyes Only (Hunter): So it's broken down into levels then. Things are locked off based on the progress you've made up until that point?

Decent Treatment (Dean): Yeah, some things are. The battles are cool in this game particularly because there aren't a ton of knobs being turned. It's really distilled into just its bare essentials. We decided we're gonna do the the temperature as an interesting mechanic, and also the enemies are able to approach from the background. So over the course of a battle, you'll maybe have a moment to prepare as the enemy is getting closer.
